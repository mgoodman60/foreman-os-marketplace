# Lighting Conditions: Complete Parameter Sets for Rendering Prompts

This guide specifies exact lighting parameters for every standard architectural rendering condition. Each condition includes time of day, color temperature, shadow characteristics, mood, best use cases, and complete prompt language for constructing accurate lighting descriptions.

---

## Exterior Lighting Conditions

### Golden Hour (Late Afternoon / Early Evening)

**Time of Day**: 3:00 PM - 7:00 PM (varies by latitude and season; roughly 1-2 hours before sunset)

**Sun Position**: Low angle from southwest (or northwest if evening), long shadows stretching northeast (or southeast)

**Color Temperature**: Warm amber-orange, approximately 3000-3500K

**Shadow Characteristics:**
- Very long shadows (2-4x object height)
- Soft shadow edges (atmospheric scattering)
- Deep shadow areas with warm reflected light
- Dramatic contrast between sunlit and shaded surfaces
- Shadow color: warm amber-orange, not gray or blue

**Mood**: Warm, inviting, nostalgic, prestigious, exclusive, comfortable

**Surface Effects**:
- Warm-toned materials appear richer and more saturated (brick, warm stone, wood)
- Glazing shows interior warm glow mingling with golden exterior reflection
- Metallic surfaces (aluminum, glass) reflect golden light creating shimmer
- Grass and vegetation appear saturated green with golden highlights
- Asphalt/concrete shows warm gray tones with golden reflection

**Best Use Cases:**
- Marketing and presentation renderings
- Residential properties
- Hospitality and resort projects
- Mixed-use with retail/dining emphasis
- Landscape emphasis with green spaces
- Prestigious corporate or institutional buildings
- Retail storefronts showing interior activity and warmth
- Entry and approach views (emphasizes welcome)
- Heritage/traditional architecture

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Golden hour late afternoon light, warm amber sun from southwest, long dramatic shadows stretching across foreground, soft edge shadows"
- "Late afternoon warm light creating rich material tones, deep golden reflections on glass, green landscape illuminated with golden highlights"
- "3:30 PM golden hour, sun low on horizon creating warm directional light, interior spaces showing warm glow through glazing, prestigious atmosphere"
- "Sunset approach lighting with warm amber-orange color temperature, long shadows from trees creating dappled patterns, inviting and warm ambiance"

**Photography Equivalent**: "Golden hour landscape photography" or "warm sunset architectural lighting"

### Midday Sun (High Overhead Light)

**Time of Day**: 11:00 AM - 2:00 PM (roughly 3 hours around solar noon)

**Sun Position**: High overhead, nearly straight up depending on latitude and season

**Color Temperature**: Neutral white, approximately 5500-6500K

**Shadow Characteristics:**
- Short shadows directly under objects
- Very sharp, high-contrast shadows with hard edges
- Deep black shadows with minimal visible detail in shadow areas
- Strong directional light emphasizing texture and detail
- Shadow color: neutral gray to slightly blue-gray

**Mood**: Bright, efficient, business-like, clear, exposing, forensic, stark, clinical

**Surface Effects**:
- All colors appear true and saturated (no color shift from warm/cool)
- High contrast between light and dark areas creates graphic appearance
- Fine details and surface texture maximized
- Glazing reflects bright sky (minimal interior visibility)
- Metallic surfaces appear bright and reflective without warm shimmer
- Grass appears fresh and green with high saturation

**Best Use Cases:**
- Technical/permit renderings
- Detail and material specification renderings
- Industrial or utilitarian buildings (clarity emphasizes function)
- Modern/contemporary architecture (high contrast aesthetic complements contemporary design)
- Daytime operational focus (offices, healthcare, schools)
- Construction documentation and phasing plans
- Site plans and overhead views (shadows define spatial relationships)
- Projects where clarity and honesty of materials is priority

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Bright midday sun from directly overhead, sharp high-contrast shadows beneath eaves and overhangs, neutral 5500K color temperature"
- "Noon sun creating graphic shadow patterns, strong directional light emphasizing surface texture, clear and exposing lighting conditions"
- "High noon bright overcast-alternative light, minimal warm tones, maximum material clarity, high-saturation colors, forensic clarity"
- "Midday sun straight overhead creating short shadows directly beneath building, bright and neutral color temperature, clinical precision"

**Photography Equivalent**: "Midday documentary photography" or "forensic clarity lighting"

**When to Use Cautiously:**
- Interior spaces appear dark and cavernous due to bright exterior glare
- Glass facades show only sky reflection, not interior activity
- Not ideal for welcoming or warm atmosphere emphasis

### Overcast / Cloudy Bright

**Time of Day**: Any time of day (but best 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM for adequate light)

**Sun Position**: Diffused through clouds; no direct shadow

**Color Temperature**: Cool neutral white, approximately 6000-6500K (slightly cool blue undertone)

**Shadow Characteristics:**
- No harsh shadows; extremely soft or completely absent
- Even ambient lighting across all surfaces
- Shadow areas still retain visible detail (not black)
- Gentle directional hint if sun is visible through thin clouds
- Shadow color: cool neutral gray to pale blue

**Mood**: Soft, professional, contemplative, calm, even-handed, accessible, democratic, sustainable

**Surface Effects**:
- All surfaces evenly illuminated; no dramatic contrast
- Colors appear natural and true without warm enhancement
- Fine surface texture evident due to soft light raking
- Glazing shows interior activity clearly (no glare reflection)
- Matte finishes appear soft; glossy finishes less dramatic
- Green vegetation appears fresh and natural

**Best Use Cases:**
- Professional renderings (most versatile and accepted)
- Healthcare and institutional buildings (neutral, trustworthy)
- Educational facilities
- Sustainable/green building emphasis (overcast associated with environmental responsibility)
- Detailed architectural renderings (soft light shows detail without drama)
- Interior connections emphasized (soft exterior allows interior to be visible)
- Material specification focus (colors appear true)
- Government and civic buildings
- Residential (calm, accessible atmosphere)
- Projects emphasizing accessibility and universal design

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Overcast bright daylight, soft diffuse light, no harsh shadows, even illumination across all surfaces, cool neutral 6500K color temperature"
- "Cloudy bright conditions, gentle ambient light with no deep shadows, soft professional appearance, detail visible in all areas"
- "Overcast sky providing uniform diffuse illumination, soft shadows with detail visible, calm and measured atmosphere"
- "Cloud-softened daylight, even light distribution, no glare or extreme contrast, interior-exterior connection visible without darkness"

**Photography Equivalent**: "Overcast daylight professional photography" or "soft natural diffuse light"

**When to Avoid:**
- Projects needing dramatic architectural expression (overcast can appear bland)
- Luxury/prestige positioning (golden hour preferred)
- Strong directional architectural elements (needs shadow to emphasize form)

### Blue Hour / Dusk / Early Evening

**Time of Day**: 15-45 minutes before sunset through 30 minutes after sunset (twilight window)

**Sun Position**: Below horizon; only sky illumination remains

**Color Temperature**: Cool blue-purple, approximately 4000-5000K exterior; warm 2700-3000K artificial interior lighting

**Shadow Characteristics:**
- No direct shadows (sun below horizon)
- Very soft directional lighting from remaining sky
- Shadow areas visible due to residual skylight
- Contrast created by interior artificial light vs. cool exterior blue
- Cool shadows with blue undertone

**Mood**: Atmospheric, romantic, mystical, exclusive, sophisticated, magical, contemplative, safe/welcoming (interior warmth vs. cool exterior)

**Surface Effects**:
- Exterior materials appear cool and subtle
- Interior spaces glow with warm artificial light (dramatic contrast)
- Glazing becomes mirror-like (exterior cool blue + interior warm reflection)
- Landscape features backlit by residual sky glow
- Architectural lighting (exterior uplighting, accent lighting) becomes prominent visual element
- Stars becoming visible if clear sky

**Best Use Cases:**
- Luxury hospitality and resort projects
- Retail and dining emphasis (interior warmth, nighttime activity)
- Exclusive residential (prestige and calm)
- Civic/institutional landmarks with architectural lighting
- Mixed-use with nighttime activity emphasis
- Marketing and presentation renderings (highly atmospheric)
- Entry and gathering spaces (warm welcome from cool exterior)
- Healthcare at night (calm, safe atmosphere)
- Corporate headquarters with strong architectural lighting program

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Blue hour dusk lighting, cool blue-purple 4500K sky, warm golden interior light glowing warmly through windows, sophisticated atmospheric mood"
- "Early evening twilight, cool exterior blue tones transitioning to warm interior illumination, dramatic contrast between cool sky and warm lit interior"
- "Dusk blue hour with warm interior glow visible through glazing, exterior accent lighting emphasizing architectural features, romantic and exclusive atmosphere"
- "Twilight conditions showing cool blue sky above, warm amber interior light creating visual warmth against cool exterior, architectural lighting design prominent"

**Photography Equivalent**: "Blue hour architectural photography" or "twilight photography with interior warm glow"

**Outdoor Lighting Parameters** (if included):
- Facade uplighting: warm 2700K color, subtle accentuation
- Path lighting: warm 2700-3000K, pedestrian-level illumination
- Entry candela: warm white, clear wayfinding
- Accent/downlighting: typically cool 4000-5000K if architectural expression focus

**When to Use Carefully:**
- Ensure artificial lighting design is actually planned; don't show lights that don't exist
- Interior spaces must have plausible interior lighting (suggest occupied building)
- Balance cool blue exterior with warm interior to avoid blue-dominant appearance

### Dawn / Early Morning

**Time of Day**: 30 minutes before sunrise through 1 hour after sunrise

**Sun Position**: Low angle from east (or southeast), very long shadows westward

**Color Temperature**: Cool blue-purple sky (4500-5500K), warm peachy-rose sun at horizon (2500-3000K)

**Shadow Characteristics:**
- Very long shadows (3-5x object height)
- Cool shadow color due to blue-dominated sky (more blue than golden hour shadows)
- Warm edge-lighting on surfaces facing east
- Soft shadow edges due to atmospheric scattering
- Morning mist or dew may diffuse light further

**Mood**: Quiet, peaceful, fresh, hopeful, serene, contemplative, sustainable (early-rising sustainability theme), mysterious

**Surface Effects**:
- Cool blue throughout most of composition
- Warm peachy-rose glow at horizon and east-facing edges
- Morning dew/moisture emphasizes natural materials (stone, wood appear enhanced)
- Grass appears fresh and green with dew
- Glazing shows cool blue sky reflection with minimal interior visibility
- Subtle color contrast between cool majority and warm horizon edge

**Best Use Cases:**
- Sustainability-focused projects (dawn = renewable energy, early hours = efficiency)
- Healthcare and wellness (fresh start, peaceful healing)
- Educational institutions (new day, fresh learning)
- Nature-integrated projects (landscape, environmental design)
- Spiritual/religious projects (renewal, hope)
- Residential emphasis on peaceful living
- Projects emphasizing fresh air, natural materials, groundedness
- Landscape and outdoor space emphasis

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Early dawn light with cool blue-purple sky, warm peachy-rose sun at horizon, long shadows stretching westward, quiet serene atmosphere"
- "Sunrise approach with cool morning sky, warm edge-lighting on east-facing surfaces, very long shadows creating texture and depth, fresh and hopeful mood"
- "Early morning before full sunrise, cool blue tones dominating with warm glow at horizon, peaceful and contemplative atmosphere, morning dew visible on landscape"
- "Dawn breaking with cool sky and warm horizon glow, long directional shadows, serene beginning-of-day quality, sustainable and fresh aesthetic"

**Photography Equivalent**: "Dawn landscape photography" or "early morning documentary photography"

**When to Use Cautiously:**
- Ensures design intent includes early-morning functionality (many buildings don't operate at dawn)
- Interior spaces appear dark; need to suggest interior lighting or daytime context instead

---

### Night / Artificial Lighting Dominant

**Time of Day**: Full night (several hours after sunset through before dawn)

**Sun Position**: Below horizon; no skylight remaining

**Color Temperature**: Entirely artificial; typically warm 2700-3000K for welcoming spaces, cool 4000K for task, mix of both for layered design

**Shadow Characteristics:**
- Shadows created only by artificial light sources
- Shadow direction depends on light fixture location (multiple shadows possible)
- Shadow characteristics depend on fixture type (soft floods, hard spotlights, etc.)
- May have minimal shadows if uplighting/general illumination dominates
- Dark areas outside light pools appear black (no residual skylight)

**Mood**: Dramatic, exclusive, glamorous, intimate, energetic, mysterious, safe (if well-lit), ominous (if dark areas present)

**Surface Effects**:
- Warm interior light dominates visual composition
- Exterior materials appear warm-toned from uplighting (if included)
- Glazing becomes opaque black reflection of interior light (no exterior view)
- Landscape features visible only if lit
- Darkness surrounding lit areas can appear threatening or exclusive depending on design
- Artificial materials (glass, metal) show specular reflections of light sources

**Best Use Cases:**
- Luxury hospitality (nighttime exclusivity and glamour)
- Fine dining and entertainment venues
- Retail and entertainment centers (nighttime activity)
- Corporate headquarters (24-hour prestige)
- Casinos and gaming (dramatic lighting)
- Nightclubs and music venues
- Healthcare (emergency department, 24-hour operations)
- Security and institutional (controlled, lit environment)
- Residential (intimate, safe home)
- Architectural lighting showcase (if facade lighting is key design feature)

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Night scene with warm interior artificial lighting glowing through windows, exterior facade accent lighting in warm 2700K, dramatic and exclusive atmosphere"
- "Evening nighttime with building strongly illuminated from within, warm amber interior light dominant, minimal exterior illumination creating focus on interior activity"
- "Night rendering with targeted artificial lighting on key features, warm welcome light at entry, dark sky and surroundings creating dramatic focus"
- "Nighttime operation with full artificial lighting scheme, warm interior glow visible through transparent glazing, cooler facade uplighting creating architectural emphasis"

**Photography Equivalent**: "Night architectural photography" or "nighttime city scene photography"

**Exterior Lighting Design Parameters** (if included):
- **Uplighting**: Warm 2700K, subtle accentuation of form
- **Facade washing**: Cool 4000K or warm 2700K depending on material and design intent
- **Accent lighting**: May be cool (4000-5000K) for dramatic effect
- **Entry lighting**: Warm 2700-3000K for welcome and security
- **Path/landscape lighting**: Warm 2700K, subtle wayfinding
- **Avoid**: Over-lit or bright-lit nighttime scenes (appears artificial and harsh); target pools of light with darkness between

**When to Use Cautiously:**
- Ensure lighting design is plausible and intentional (not just dark building with lights randomly placed)
- Interior spaces need plausible occupancy or operational logic
- Avoid total darkness; even nighttime scenes need some ambient light or starlight
- Architectural/accent lighting should accentuate design intent, not distract

### Storm Approaching / Dramatic Sky

**Time of Day**: Typically afternoon (2:00 PM - 5:00 PM) but can be any time during storm conditions

**Sun Position**: Obscured by storm clouds; directional light still present from breaks in cloud cover

**Color Temperature**: Cool gray-green-purple, approximately 5000-6000K; can shift to yellow-green before tornado conditions

**Shadow Characteristics:**
- Directional shadows possible from sun breaks in clouds
- Generally soft but stronger contrast than overcast
- Dark areas under cloud shadow may be quite deep
- Shadows may shift in direction as clouds move
- Shadow color: cool neutral to slight green-gray tone

**Mood**: Dramatic, ominous, energetic, dynamic, tense, awe-inspiring, threatening, powerful, sublime

**Surface Effects**:
- High contrast between lit areas and dark cloud shadows
- Colors may shift toward green or gray-purple
- Glazing may show reflected sky turbulence
- Landscape elements may appear backlit by breaks in cloud
- Wind effects (trees bending) can be suggested
- Moisture in air (visible in photo renderings) can add drama

**Best Use Cases:**
- Modern/contemporary architecture (drama suits style)
- Disaster-resilient or climate-responsive design
- Environmental or sustainability emphasis (climate change theme)
- Drama in presentation (attention-grabbing)
- Extreme sports or high-energy venues
- Horror or tension-themed projects (rare but possible)
- Contrast emphasis (building stands strong against elements)
- Landscape/site resilience emphasis

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Storm approaching with dark purple-gray clouds overhead, strong directional light from cloud breaks creating dramatic contrast, ominous and powerful atmosphere"
- "Dramatic threatening sky with greenish undertones, high contrast between illuminated surfaces and deep shadow areas from storm clouds, dynamic and energetic mood"
- "Pre-storm conditions with dark clouds and dramatic light breaks, green-gray color cast, wind-blown vegetation, sublime and awe-inspiring atmosphere"
- "Severe weather approaching with turbulent sky and strong directional light creating stark contrast, ominous and powerful aesthetic"

**Photography Equivalent**: "Storm photography" or "dramatic weather landscape photography"

**When to Use Cautiously:**
- Ensure design intent supports dramatic/tense mood
- Can overshadow design qualities with excess drama
- Not suitable for welcoming or peaceful buildings
- May appear apocalyptic rather than architectural unless carefully handled

## Interior Lighting Conditions

### Ambient Fluorescent / Institutional Lighting

**Light Source**: Ceiling-mounted fluorescent or LED troffers in 2x2 or 2x4 configuration

**Color Temperature**: Cool white or neutral, 4000K (can be warm 3000K or cool 5000K depending on specification)

**Light Distribution**: Diffuse, even, minimal shadow

**Fixture Visibility**: Visible in ceiling grid, plastic or metal diffuser visible

**Surface Effects**:
- Even, uniform illumination across all surfaces
- Minimal texture emphasis (diffuse light flattens surfaces)
- Institutional appearance (schools, offices, hospitals)
- Slightly cool or neutral overall tone
- Minimal shadow; visible detail everywhere

**Best Use Cases:**
- Healthcare facilities (required for visibility and institutional context)
- Educational institutions (teaching requires even illumination)
- Office environments (task-focused work spaces)
- Industrial/warehouse (functional efficiency)
- Institutional/government buildings
- Technical/specification renderings (shows true colors)

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Institutional overhead fluorescent lighting in 2x2 ceiling troffers, cool 4000K white light, even diffuse illumination, minimal shadows, clinical professional appearance"
- "Standard office ambient lighting with recessed fluorescent fixtures, uniform brightness, cool-neutral color temperature, even workspace illumination"

**When to Use Cautiously:**
- Can appear institutional or cold if that's not intended
- Minimize in luxury or high-end residential contexts
- Supplement with task or accent lighting for more dynamic interiors

---

### Recessed Downlights (LED / Incandescent)

**Light Source**: Ceiling-recessed downlights, typically 4-6 inch diameter

**Color Temperature**: Warm 2700K (most common) or neutral 3000-4000K

**Light Distribution**: Directional cone of light; creates pools of light and shadow areas

**Fixture Appearance**: Small recessed openings in ceiling; diffuse trim ring visible

**Surface Effects**:
- Directional pools of light on surfaces below
- Moderate shadow areas between pools
- Warm 2700K creates intimate atmosphere
- Surface texture emphasized by directional light
- Creates visual hierarchy with lit areas prominent

**Best Use Cases:**
- Residential interiors (warm and intimate)
- Hospitality (inviting and comfortable)
- Retail and dining (draws attention to features)
- Galleries and display spaces
- Luxury and high-end interiors
- Intimate and social spaces
- Accent lighting for features

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Recessed downlight ambient lighting with warm 2700K incandescent/LED, pools of light creating visual warmth, pools and shadows creating depth and texture"
- "Ceiling recessed lights with warm directional light pools, intimate atmosphere, surface texture emphasized by angled light"

**When to Use Cautiously:**
- Ensure spacing is reasonable (not too sparse or creating harsh spots)
- Avoid too many lights creating a grid-like appearance if not intended
- Test visibility of surfaces in shadow areas

---

### Cove / Indirect Lighting

**Light Source**: Hidden lighting source in cove or soffit, providing indirect ambient light

**Color Temperature**: Typically warm 2700K or neutral 3000K

**Light Distribution**: Soft diffuse light reflecting off ceiling or wall surfaces

**Fixture Appearance**: Hidden; only reflected light visible on ceiling or wall

**Surface Effects**:
- Very soft, shadow-free or minimal shadow
- Warm ambient glow from ceiling or upper wall
- Inviting and sophisticated appearance
- All surfaces visible but with soft quality
- Emphasis on surface color rather than texture

**Best Use Cases:**
- Luxury hospitality and residential
- Galleries and high-end retail
- Elegant dining
- Healthcare (calming and soft)
- Sophisticated commercial interiors
- Entry vestibules and lobbies
- Feature wall or architectural detail emphasis

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Cove indirect lighting providing soft ambient glow, warm 2700K color from hidden source, sophisticated and inviting atmosphere with minimal shadows"
- "Indirect ceiling-bounce lighting creating soft even illumination, warm and welcoming quality, no visible shadow or harsh directional light"

---

### Task Lighting (Under-Cabinet, Desk, etc.)

**Light Source**: Under-cabinet, under-shelf, or desk-mounted task lights; typically LED

**Color Temperature**: Warm 2700K (residential) or neutral 3000-4000K (commercial)

**Light Distribution**: Concentrated light on work surface, with spill light illuminating area beyond

**Fixture Appearance**: Visible at edge of cabinet or desk, small discrete source

**Surface Effects**:
- Bright illumination on task surface (counter, desk, shelf)
- Falloff creating gradient from bright work area to ambient surround
- Warm directional light on work surface
- Surface under task light appears highlighted and important
- Helps define spatial hierarchy and function

**Best Use Cases:**
- Kitchen and bathroom renderings (countertop emphasis)
- Office and workspace (desk task lighting)
- Retail display and merchandising
- Residential living spaces (layer with ambient light)
- Gallery and museum displays
- Jewelry and fine retail

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Under-cabinet task lighting in warm 2700K, bright illumination on counter surface, spill light creating functional ambiance in kitchen space"
- "Desk-mounted warm task lighting emphasizing work surface, gradient falloff to surrounding ambient light, functional and focused atmosphere"

---

### Wall Wash / Accent Lighting

**Light Source**: Recessed or track-mounted uplights or downlights focused on wall surface

**Color Temperature**: Can vary; warm 2700K for feature emphasis, cool 4000-5000K for architectural detail, or color (RGB) for dramatic effect

**Light Distribution**: Light directed at feature surface, creating gradient from bright at top to darker at bottom (or vice versa for uplighting)

**Fixture Appearance**: Typically hidden; only light effect visible on wall

**Surface Effects**:
- Featured wall or surface appears bright and important
- Texture of wall material emphasized by angled light
- Surrounding walls darker by contrast
- Creates visual hierarchy and spatial focus
- Can emphasize material quality, texture, or color

**Best Use Cases:**
- Accent wall emphasis (color or material feature)
- Artwork or display emphasis
- Stone or textured wall emphasis
- Entry feature walls (welcome emphasis)
- Architectural detail emphasis
- Gallery and museum lighting
- Luxury retail displays
- Hospitality feature emphasis

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Wall-wash accent lighting emphasizing stone feature wall, warm 2700K directional light creating shadow texture, focal point prominence"
- "Downlit accent lighting on textured accent wall, grazing light emphasizing surface detail and material character, visual hierarchy created"

---

### Window / Natural Light

**Light Source**: Daylight from windows; quality depends on exterior lighting condition

**Color Temperature**: Varies with time of day (golden hour warm, midday neutral, overcast cool)

**Light Distribution**: Depends on window size and exterior conditions; can be directional or diffuse

**Fixture Appearance**: Window glazing and frame visible; interior may be backlit or sidelite depending on orientation

**Surface Effects**:
- Surfaces facing window brightly illuminated
- Interior glow from daylight creates connection to exterior
- Glazing may show exterior view or reflect interior spaces
- Warm or cool tint depending on exterior lighting condition
- Shadows on interior surfaces cast by window frame mullions

**Best Use Cases:**
- Rendering entire interior daytime scenes
- Connection to exterior and views
- Healthcare and wellness emphasis (daylight = health)
- Modern/contemporary with extensive glazing
- Natural-light-focused design
- Almost all daytime interior renderings

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Large window with bright daylight streaming into space, warm afternoon light creating interior illumination and shadow patterns, natural ventilation and daylighting emphasized"
- "Daytime interior with window-sourced natural light, golden hour warm tones visible through glazing, interior-exterior connection and daylighting quality emphasized"
- "Glazing showing blue overcast sky, diffuse even natural light illuminating interior surfaces, daylighting and window connection prominent design feature"

---

### Layered Interior Lighting (Ambient + Task + Accent)

**Light Sources**: Multiple lighting types working together (recessed ambient, task lights, accent wall, natural light)

**Color Temperature**: Typically warm 2700K (creates unified warm interior despite multiple sources)

**Light Distribution**: Layered and complex; bright areas (task, accent, window) and softer ambient areas

**Fixture Appearance**: Multiple types visible or hidden depending on design; creating visual interest and sophistication

**Surface Effects**:
- Complex visual hierarchy with multiple focal points
- Warm overall tone unifies diverse light sources
- Texture and detail visible throughout (bright area for detail, softer areas for ambiance)
- Sophisticated and professionally lit appearance
- Realistic interior scene with natural light layers

**Best Use Cases:**
- High-end residential and hospitality
- Retail and commercial showrooms
- Professional office environments
- Galleries and museums
- Restaurants and fine dining
- Healthcare and wellness spaces
- Any sophisticated interior requiring visual interest

**Lighting Prompt Language:**
- "Layered interior lighting with warm recessed ambient light, bright task lighting on desk surface, accent wall uplight, and golden daylight from windows, sophisticated professional appearance"
- "Multipart lighting scheme with warm 2700K ambient recessed lights, bright task lighting on counter, natural window light, creating visual depth and professional rendering quality"

## Advanced Lighting Parameters

### Time-Lapse / Multiple Lighting Conditions
For projects showing how building performs at different times, specify conditions sequentially:

**Example Prompt Language:**
- "Series of renderings showing morning blue hour transition to midday sun, then golden hour late afternoon, then blue hour evening, displaying daylighting effectiveness throughout day"

### Seasonal Lighting Variations
Latitude affects sun angle; specify if season matters:

**Example Prompt Language:**
- "Summer midday sun nearly straight overhead with minimal shadows, demonstrating summer heat gain challenge"
- "Winter low-angle afternoon sun, long shadows demonstrating winter solar gain benefit"

### Artificial Lighting Design Intent
Always specify warm color temperature (2700K) as default for institutional/healthcare settings require 4000K:

**Example Prompt Language:**
- "Interior ambient lighting in warm 2700K creating inviting residential atmosphere" (vs.)
- "Interior ambient lighting in cool 4000K creating clinical professional atmosphere" (healthcare)
